Introduction

Lamine Yamal Nasraoui Ebana (born 13 July 2007 in Esplugas de Llobregat) is a Spanish footballer who plays as a striker for FC Barcelona in the Spanish Primera Division.

On 26 October 2024, he became the youngest player to score in a classic against Real Madrid, in a 4–0 win at the Santiago Bernabeu, surpassing Ansu Fati's mark.

He is the youngest player in history to make his debut and score a goal for the Spanish national team
  • Debut: September 8, 2023
Trajectory

He grew up in the youth ranks of La Masia and was soon considered one of the best promises in the academy.

He was incorporated into the Juvenil A team, already above his age level, in the 2022-23 season and was selected by Xavi Hernández to train with the first team along with other youth players at the beginning of September 2022.

Premio Golden Boy - The Youngest 2023
  • Even without having signed his first professional contract with the club, he was one of the youth players who most impressed the Catalan coach.​
Style of play

Known for his dribbling, speed, pace, technical ability and vision, he is widely regarded as one of the best young footballers in the world.

Yamal has also cited former Barcelona player Neymar as a major influence on his own style of play.

He is a left-footed striker with great dribbling, passing and scoring skills, who can play both as a centre forward and as an attacking midfielder or winger, mainly on the right wing.
  • With his technical profile, he was compared to former Barcelona footballer Lionel Messi, like many La Masia players before him.
Early life

He was born in Esplugas de Llobregat, the son of Mounir Nasraoui, a Moroccan, and Sheila Ebana, an Equatoguinean.

he was raised in Rocafonda, a working-class neighbourhood of Mataró.

He spent most of his youth in Barcelona.

National team

Lamine Yamal has been a youth international with Spain. In 2021 he played four games and scored one goal for Spain U-16s. He also played for the U-15s in 2022, with whom he proved to be a prolific goalscorer.

On 9 July 2024, during the semi-final match against France, he became the youngest player to score at a European Championship, at the age of 16 years and 362 days.
